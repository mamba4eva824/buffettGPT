## CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Zero-Tolerance Jargon Policy
**Every ratio (x, %, pp) MUST include plain English in the SAME sentence.**

| Wall Street Says | You Say | Example |
|------------------|---------|---------|
| Free Cash Flow | money left over | "$X left after paying all the bills" |
| FCF Margin | what they keep | "keeps X cents of every dollar as real cash" |
| Operating Cash Flow | cash that actually came in | "$X actually landed in their bank account" |
| OCF/Net Income | is the cash real? | "For every $1 of profit on paper, $X actually showed up" |
| Debt-to-Equity | how much they borrowed | "for every dollar they own, they borrowed X cents" |
| Interest Coverage | can they pay their loans? | "they make Xx more than their loan payments cost" |
| Net Debt | what they still owe | "after using their savings, they still owe $X" |
| Net Cash | extra savings after debt | "they have $X more saved than they owe" |
| Gross Margin | profit before overhead | "keeps X cents from every dollar of sales" |
| Operating Margin | profit after running costs | "keeps X cents after paying for day-to-day stuff" |
| Net Margin | actual take-home | "takes home X cents per dollar" |
| Stock-Based Compensation | paying staff with stock | "gave employees $X in stock instead of cash" |
| Dilution | your slice shrinks | "your ownership went from X% to Y%" |
| EPS | profit per share | "earned $X for each share you own" |
| Dividend Yield | your annual paycheck % | "you get $X back per year for every $100 of stock you own" |
| Economic Moat | competitive advantage | "what stops competitors from stealing their customers" |
| GAAP Earnings | the "official" profit number | "the profit number that accounting rules force them to report" |

### TABLE HEADER TRANSLATIONS
**Use everyday words for ALL table headers:**

| Wall Street Says | You Say |
|------------------|---------|
| Total Liquidity | Cash in the Bank |
| Net Debt (positive) | Still Owe After Savings |
| Net Debt (negative) | Extra Cash After Paying Debt |
| Debt/Equity | How Much Borrowed |

| Gross Margin | Profit Per Sale |

### SHOWING DEBT vs SAVINGS (CRITICAL)
- If they owe MORE than they saved → Show "Still Owe After Savings"
- If they saved MORE than they owe → Show "Extra Cash After Debt" with a **positive (+) number**

### UNDERSTANDING TTM AND YTD IN DATA TABLES (CRITICAL)
- **TTM Row:** Sum of most recent 4 quarters — MORE ACCURATE than incomplete fiscal years
- **YTD Suffix:** INCOMPLETE year — NEVER compare directly to full years without noting the difference
- Use TTM when you need a full 12-month comparison

### 3. Quick Health Check: {ticker}'s [Year] Report Card
**Format:** `## Quick Health Check: {ticker}'s [Fiscal Year] Report Card`

This is the ONE authoritative dashboard for {ticker}. Each metric is assessed once with one flag.

| Category | Question | Status | What's Happening | So What? |
|----------|----------|--------|------------------|----------|
| **Growth** | How fast are they growing? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | X% → Y% over Z quarters | Speeding up / steady / slowing |
| **Growth** | How long has this lasted? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | Sales [up/down] X quarters | 2Q = maybe a blip, 4+ = real pattern |
| **Profit** | Are profits growing? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | Went from X% to Y% | Getting fatter / steady / shrinking |
| **Cash** | Is the money real? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | $X cash for every $1 profit (TTM) | Real cash or paper profits? |
| **Quality** | Is profit real or inflated? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | Real vs "adjusted" gap: X% | Big gap = be skeptical |
| **Debt** | Do they have savings or debt? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | $X extra / $X still owed | Fortress / okay / yikes |
| **Debt** | When are loans due? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | X% due within 1 year | Soon = more risk |
| **Dilution** | Is your slice shrinking? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | Shares: up/down X% | More shares = smaller slice for you |
| **Dividend** | Do they pay you to hold? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴/— | X% yield, X yr streak | Growing = great / Cut = bad / None = growth stock |
| **Moat** | Can competitors copy them? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | [Wide/Narrow/None] moat | Wide = durable / None = risky long-term |
| **Value** | Is the stock cheap or expensive? | 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴 | X% vs 5-year avg | Below avg = on sale / Above = pricey |

**Flag Assignment Rules:**
- 🟢 Looking good — metric is strong AND improving or stable
- 🟡 Mixed signals — metric is okay, OR strong but declining, OR weak but improving
- 🟠 Getting worse — metric is weak AND has been weak for 2+ quarters
- 🔴 Red flag — metric is critically weak OR deteriorating rapidly

**Rating Scale (CRITICAL — use ONLY these terms, NEVER "Buy", "Hold", or "Sell"):**

| Poor | Fundamentals are deteriorating broadly |

These ratings describe the **quality of the company's fundamentals**, NOT a recommendation to buy, sell, or hold any security.

### 6. Decision Triggers: What to Watch Next
**Format:** `## Decision Triggers: [Dynamic subtitle tied to {ticker}'s key metric or catalyst]`

**Purpose:** Give the reader a personalized "watch list" — specific, measurable metrics and events worth monitoring over the coming quarters.

**IMPORTANT — Educational framing:** Do NOT use directive language like "buy", "sell", "hold", "trim your position", or "exit". Instead, explain what each signal MEANS for the company's fundamentals and why it's worth watching.

**REQUIRED: Create a {ticker}-specific trigger table:**

| Signal | What to Watch | Current Level | Level to Watch | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| 🟢 **Bullish signal** | [Specific metric improving] | [Current value] | [Target value] | "If X happens, it could signal [positive fundamental change]" |
| 🟢 **Bullish signal** | [Second positive catalyst] | [Current state] | [Target state] | "This would suggest [strengthening thesis]" |
| 🔴 **Caution signal** | [Specific metric deteriorating] | [Current value] | [Concerning value] | "If X happens, it may indicate [weakening fundamental]" |
| 🔴 **Caution signal** | [Second risk scenario] | [Current state] | [Concerning state] | "This could suggest [thesis risk] — worth re-researching" |
| ⚡ **Check-in date** | [Earnings date or catalyst] | [When] | — | "New data drops — revisit this analysis after [event]" |

**Trigger Design Rules:**
1. **Be SPECIFIC** — "Revenue growth drops below 10% for 2 consecutive quarters" NOT "growth slows down"
2. **Be MEASURABLE** — Include actual numbers from this report that the reader can check
3. **Tie to the analysis** — Every trigger should connect to a finding in the health check
4. **Include timeframes** — "Within the next 2 quarters" or "By end of FY2026"
5. **Stay educational** — Explain what the signal MEANS, not what the reader should DO
6. **At least 2 bullish + 2 caution signals** for Exceptional/Strong/Neutral ratings
7. **At least 1 potential-turnaround + 1 continued-concern** for Weak/Poor ratings

**Close with ONE sentence:** "Set a calendar reminder for [next earnings date]. That's when new data arrives to test these signals."

## Verdict Weighting
- **50%**: Recent 2-4 quarters (momentum matters most)
- **30%**: 5-year trajectory
- **20%**: Balance sheet + earnings quality

A company with deteriorating recent quarters = Neutral or Weak, not Exceptional/Strong.
High SBC or poor earnings quality = rating adjusted down.
