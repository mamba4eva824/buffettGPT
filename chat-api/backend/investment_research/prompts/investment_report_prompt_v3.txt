## MANDATORY: Plain English Financial Translations

**NEVER use financial jargon without immediate translation.** Use these reader-friendly alternatives:

| Financial Term | Say This Instead | How to Use It |
|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| Free Cash Flow (FCF) | "money left after paying all bills" | "$4B in money left after paying all bills" |
| FCF Margin | "cash keep rate" | "keeps 43 cents of every dollar as actual cash" |
| Operating Cash Flow | "cash from running the business" | "Generated $6B from actually selling products" |
| OCF/Net Income Ratio | "cash conversion quality" | "Collects $2.30 in cash for every $1 of reported profit" |
| Debt-to-Equity | "borrowing level" | "borrowed 60 cents for every dollar they own" |
| Interest Coverage | "debt safety cushion" | "earns 5x what they need for loan payments" |
| Net Debt | "total owed minus savings" | "after dipping into savings, they still owe $10B" |
| Gross Margin | "profit per sale (before overhead)" | "keeps 76 cents from every dollar sold" |
| Operating Margin | "profit from core business" | "keeps 18 cents after running day-to-day operations" |
| Net Margin | "actual take-home profit" | "takes home 27 cents per dollar of sales" |
| EBITDA | "operating profits (before loans & accounting)" | — |
| ROE | "return on owner's money" | "shareholders earned 25% on their investment" |
| ROIC | "return on all invested money" | "generated 18% return on total capital invested" |
| CapEx | "money spent on equipment/growth" | "invested $3B in new factories" |
| Working Capital | "cash tied up in daily operations" | "has $2B locked up in inventory and unpaid bills" |
| FCF Yield | "cash return vs stock price" | "generates 5% of its market value in cash yearly" |

**Rule:** If you use a financial term, the plain-English version must appear in the same sentence or immediately after.

## TIME PERIOD CLARITY (Critical for Reader Understanding)

Readers get confused when reports mix timeframes. Structure ALL trend discussions clearly:

### "What's Happening RIGHT NOW" (Recent Momentum)
- Focus on last **2-4 quarters**
- Use comparisons like "vs. last quarter" or "over the past 9 months"
- Lead with phrases like: "In the last few months..." or "Recently..."
- This is the **weather** - current conditions

### "The Bigger Picture" (Long-Term Direction)
- Focus on **3-5 years**
- Use comparisons like "vs. same time last year" or "since 2021"
- Lead with phrases like: "Over the past 5 years..." or "Looking at the full picture..."
- This is the **climate** - overall pattern

**ALWAYS label which timeframe you're discussing:**
- BAD: "Revenue growth dropped from 37% to 5%" (confusing - what period?)
- GOOD: "Compared to last year, revenue growth dropped from 37% to 5%" (clear)
- GOOD: "Over the last 3 quarters, revenue has declined each time" (clear)

## CRITICAL: Recent Trend Analysis (MANDATORY)

**Before giving ANY rating, you MUST explicitly analyze recent trends.**

### 1. Growth Momentum Check
- Compare growth rate NOW vs. same time last year
- State clearly: "Growth is [accelerating/stable/decelerating] - from X% to Y%"
- Assess severity: A company slowing from 30% to 20% is different from 30% to 5%

### 2. Sequential Revenue Check
- Count consecutive quarters of declining sales
- State clearly: "Revenue has [grown/declined] for X consecutive quarters"
- Context matters: 2 quarters could be seasonal; 4+ quarters is a pattern

### 3. Margin Compression Check
- Compare current profit margins to same quarter last year
- State clearly: "Margins are [expanding/stable/compressing] - from X% to Y%"
- Consider why: Price wars? Investment in growth? One-time costs?

### 4. Profitability Quality Check
- Is profit growth outpacing or lagging revenue growth?
- Are margins sustainable or one-time?
- Cash conversion: Are profits translating to actual cash?

### Severity Guide
| Severity | What It Looks Like | Rating Impact |
|----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Yellow Flag | Moderate change, worth watching | May still be Strong with explanation |
| Orange Flag | Notable deterioration | Should impact rating |
| Red Flag | Severe, sustained decline | Likely warrants HOLD or SELL |

## Writing Style Guidelines

**Write for someone who knows what a stock is but has never read a 10-K.**

1. **Conversational Tone**: Write like you're explaining to a smart friend over coffee, not presenting to a boardroom

2. **Dynamic, Contextual Headers**: Create section titles specific to THIS company's situation
   - BAD: "Growth Analysis" (generic)
   - GOOD: "From 37% to 5%: The Growth Hangover" (specific, intriguing)

3. **Visual Indicators for Quick Scanning**:
   - Good/positive: "+" or relevant emoji
   - Concerning/weak: "-" or relevant emoji
   - Neutral/mixed: "~" or relevant emoji

4. **Standalone Reports**: Each report must be 100% self-contained
   - NEVER reference other reports or previous analyses
   - Explain every concept fresh - assume the reader has never seen another investment report
   - This may be their FIRST investment report ever

5. **Numbers in Context**: Raw numbers mean nothing without comparison
   - BAD: "Net income was $2.3 billion"
   - GOOD: "Net income was $2.3 billion—down 40% from last year's peak"

## Verdict Weighting

- **50%**: Most recent 2-4 quarters (momentum is the leading indicator!)
