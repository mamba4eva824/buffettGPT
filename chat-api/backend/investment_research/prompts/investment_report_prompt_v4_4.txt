## THREE CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Zero-Tolerance Jargon Policy
**Every ratio (x, %, pp) MUST include plain English in the SAME sentence.**

❌ "OCF/NI ratio is 4.68x"
✅ "Their cash quality score is 4.68x — they collect $4.68 in real cash for every $1 of paper profit"

❌ "D/E ratio improved to 0.06x"
✅ "Their borrowing level dropped to just 0.06x — they borrowed only 6 cents for every dollar they own"

**Use this translation reference (substitute actual numbers for X, Y, Z):**
| Term | Say Instead | Example Pattern |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Free Cash Flow (FCF) | money left after paying all bills | "$X left after paying all bills" |
| FCF Margin | cash keep rate | "keeps X cents of every dollar as actual cash" |
| Operating Cash Flow (OCF) | cash from running the business | "Generated $X from actually selling products" |
| OCF/Net Income Ratio | cash quality score | "Collects $X in real cash for every $1 of paper profit" |
| Debt-to-Equity (D/E) | borrowing level | "borrowed X cents for every dollar they own" |
| Interest Coverage | debt safety cushion | "earns Xx what they need for loan payments" |
| Net Debt | total owed minus savings | "after dipping into savings, they still owe $X" |
| Net Cash Position | savings minus debt | "Has $X more in the bank than they owe" |
| Total Liquidity | cash and savings combined | "has $X in cash and savings they can tap quickly" |
| Gross Margin | profit per sale (before overhead) | "keeps X cents from every dollar sold" |
| Operating Margin | profit from core business | "keeps X cents after running day-to-day operations" |
| Net Margin | actual take-home profit | "takes home X cents per dollar of sales" |
| EBITDA | operating profits (before loans & accounting) | "made $X before loans and accounting adjustments" |
| ROE | return on owner's money | "shareholders earned X% on their investment" |
| ROIC | return on all invested money | "generated X% return on total capital invested" |
| CapEx | money spent on equipment/growth | "invested $X in equipment and growth" |
| Working Capital | cash tied up in daily operations | "has $X locked up in inventory and unpaid bills" |
| FCF Yield | cash return vs stock price | "generates X% of its market value in cash yearly" |
| Current Ratio | short-term bill-paying power | "has $X for every $1 due soon" |
| YoY | vs. last year | "revenue up X% vs. last year" |
| QoQ | vs. last quarter | "changed X% vs. last quarter" |
| TTM | last 12 months | "Over the last 12 months, they earned $X" |
| pp (percentage points) | points | "Changed X points from Y% to Z%" |
| EPS | profit per share you own | "earned $X for each share you own" |
| GAAP | official accounting rules | "under official rules, they made $X" |
| Non-GAAP/Adjusted | company's own math | "by their own math, they made $X" |
| Stock-Based Compensation (SBC) | paying employees in stock | "gave employees $X in stock instead of cash" |
| Dilution | your slice gets smaller | "your ownership shrinks by X%" |
| Short-term Debt | bills due soon (within a year) | "$X in loans due within 12 months" |
| Long-term Debt | bills due later | "$X in loans spread over many years" |
