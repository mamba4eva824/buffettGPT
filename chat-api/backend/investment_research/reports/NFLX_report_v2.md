## What Does Netflix Actually Do?

**3. Live Events and Sports Are Coming**

**4. Balance Sheet Is a Fortress**
With a 0.54x borrowing level, $9.6B cash, 29.7x interest coverage, and nearly $10B in annual free cash flow, Netflix could weather almost any storm. They could buy a major studio, survive a recession, or ramp up content spending—all without breaking a sweat.

## Why You Might Want to Pass: The Bear Case for Netflix

### ⚠️ What Could Go Wrong

**1. Valuation Isn't Cheap**
At current prices, Netflix trades at roughly 35-40x next year's profit per share you own (EPS). That's expensive for a company growing 14-16%. If growth disappoints even slightly, the stock could get hit hard. You're paying for perfection.

**2. The Easy Gains Might Be Over**
Password crackdown gains are largely complete. Ad tier is scaling but faces fierce competition from YouTube, Amazon, and connected TV players. The low-hanging fruit has been picked—future growth will be harder to find.

**3. Content Costs Keep Rising**
Netflix is spending $18 billion on content in 2025, up from $17 billion in 2024. Hits like Squid Game are becoming rarer. A few expensive flops could pressure margins. Live sports rights (NFL, etc.) don't come cheap either.

**4. Competition Never Sleeps**
Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, Apple TV+, and YouTube are all well-funded and fighting for eyeballs. Amazon has unlimited resources. Disney has unmatched IP. Apple doesn't care about profits. Netflix can't afford to rest.
