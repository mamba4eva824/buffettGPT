# Deep Research Prompt: Market Intelligence Chatbot — Competitive & Market Analysis

Copy the prompt below into Claude's Deep Research mode.

---

## Prompt

I'm building a consumer-facing financial AI chatbot called **"Market Intelligence"** as a premium feature ($10/month) within an existing Warren Buffett-themed investment advisor app. I need comprehensive market research to validate the product, refine positioning, and identify risks.

### What the product does

Market Intelligence is a conversational AI interface where users ask natural-language questions about S&P 500 companies and get back structured data responses (tables, rankings, comparisons, trend charts). It's powered by an AI agent with 9 specialized tools that query a proprietary database of 5 years (20 quarters) of financial data for all ~500 S&P 500 companies.

**Example queries users can make:**
- "Top 10 companies by free cash flow margin"
- "Compare AAPL vs MSFT vs GOOGL profitability"
- "Which sectors have improving margins?"
- "How has NVDA's operating margin changed over 5 years?"
- "Companies with >30% operating margin in technology"
- "Biggest earnings surprises this quarter"
- "S&P 500 index health overview"

**Key differentiators we believe we have:**
- Natural language interface (no SQL, no screener forms)
- Covers the full S&P 500 with 20 quarters of depth per company
- Structured data rendered visually (tables, charts), not just text responses
- Bundled with existing investment report + follow-up Q&A features
- $10/month price point (significantly cheaper than Bloomberg, FactSet, etc.)

### What I need researched

**1. Competitive Landscape**

Identify and analyze direct and adjacent competitors across these categories:

- **AI-powered financial chat tools**: ChatGPT with financial plugins, Google Gemini financial features, Perplexity Finance, FinChat.io, Finchat by Stratosphere, Daloopa, any others
- **Traditional stock screeners with AI features**: Finviz, Stock Rover, TradingView, Seeking Alpha, Morningstar, Simply Wall St, Wisesheets
- **Bloomberg/terminal alternatives for retail**: Koyfin, Atom Finance (now part of Stocktwits), Stockanalysis.com, Macroaxis, GuruFocus, TIKR Terminal
- **AI financial assistants/copilots**: Copilot by Microsoft for finance, any startup financial AI assistants, robo-advisors with AI chat features
- **Free data sources**: Yahoo Finance, Google Finance, Macrotrends, SEC EDGAR AI tools

For each significant competitor, I need:
- What data they cover (just US? Global? How many years of history?)
- Whether they have natural language / conversational interfaces
- Whether they render structured data or just text
- Pricing tiers (free, premium, enterprise)
- Target audience (retail investors, professionals, institutions)
- Funding/traction if available (users, revenue, growth)
- Key strengths and weaknesses relative to my product

**2. Market Size & Opportunity**

- Total addressable market (TAM) for retail financial data/analytics tools in the US
- Serviceable addressable market (SAM) for AI-powered financial chat tools
- Number of self-directed retail investors in the US who actively research stocks
- What percentage of retail investors currently pay for financial data tools
- Average spend on financial data subscriptions among paying retail investors
- Growth trends in AI-powered financial tools (2023-2026)
- Growth trends in retail investing and self-directed investing

**3. Pricing Analysis**

- What are comparable products charging? Build a pricing comparison table
- Is $10/month the right price point? What does the data suggest?
- What's the price sensitivity for retail investors on data tools?
- Are there successful examples of financial AI tools at the $5-15/month range?
- What features justify premium pricing in this space?
- Freemium vs paid-only: what do successful competitors do?
- Trial conversion rates in financial SaaS (if data available)

**4. User Behavior & Demand Signals**

- What financial questions do retail investors most commonly ask? (Search trend data, forum analysis)
- How are people currently using ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini for stock research? What queries do they run?
- What are the top complaints about existing stock screeners and financial data tools?
- Reddit (r/investing, r/stocks, r/valueinvesting), Twitter/X, and forum sentiment about AI financial tools
- What features do users say they'd pay for vs expect for free?
- User retention patterns for financial subscription products

**5. Risks & Moats**

- **Commoditization risk**: How easily can ChatGPT/Gemini replicate this with web search or plugins? What's our defensibility?
- **Data freshness**: How critical is real-time data vs quarterly data for our target users?
- **Regulatory considerations**: Any SEC or FINRA concerns with AI-generated financial analysis? Disclaimer requirements?
- **Liability**: What disclaimers do competitors use? "Not financial advice" standards?
- **Moat analysis**: What can we build that's genuinely defensible? Proprietary data, UX quality, community, vertical integration?

**6. Go-to-Market Insights**

- How do successful financial AI tools acquire users? (SEO, content marketing, community, partnerships?)
- Which distribution channels work best for $10/month financial products?
- What positioning resonates with retail investors? (Democratizing data? AI analyst? Simple language?)
- Are there partnership opportunities (brokerages, financial advisors, media)?
- What's the typical customer acquisition cost (CAC) for financial SaaS products?

### Output format

Please structure the research as:
1. **Executive Summary** (key findings in 5-7 bullet points)
2. **Competitive Landscape** (table format + analysis)
3. **Market Size & Opportunity** (with cited numbers)
4. **Pricing Analysis** (comparison table + recommendation)
5. **User Behavior & Demand** (with evidence)
6. **Risks & Defensibility** (ranked by severity)
7. **Go-to-Market Recommendations** (actionable, prioritized)
8. **Strategic Recommendation** (build/pivot/kill assessment with reasoning)

Cite sources wherever possible. Prefer recent data (2024-2026). Flag any numbers that are estimates vs confirmed data points.
