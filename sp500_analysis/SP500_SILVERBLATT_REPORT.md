**Reading the data:** A revenue A/D ratio above 1.0 means more companies are growing than shrinking. An earnings ratio above 1.0 means more companies are improving profitability.

The story: Revenue breadth has been solid through most of this cycle — Q2-Q3 2025 posted the strongest ratios (3.23 and 2.93), with 3-to-1 advancers-to-decliners. This confirms the rally is **not just the Mag 7** — the majority of the index is growing revenue.

Earnings breadth tells a more cautious story. The net income A/D ratio has hovered near 1.0 throughout 2025, with Q4 2025 essentially flat (-4 net). This divergence — strong revenue breadth but thin earnings breadth — suggests **margin compression is spreading** even as top-line growth remains healthy. Companies are growing but not all are translating it to the bottom line.

The cumulative revenue A/D line has been in a steady uptrend since 2021, reaching +2,579 by year-end 2025. Every quarter except Q1 2024 and Q1 2025 posted positive net advances — the Q1 seasonal dips are a recurring pattern rather than a structural concern.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Buybacks remain the dominant shareholder return channel.** At ~$980B annually, buybacks outpace dividends ($641B) by a wide margin. The "Top 20 Rule" holds: 50% of buyback dollars come from 20 companies, led by Apple's $66B TTM spend.

2. **SBC dilution is the hidden cost.** Technology spent $404B on buybacks in 2025 but gave back $153B in stock-based compensation — a 38% offset. Investors should look at net buybacks, not gross.

3. **Share count reduction is accelerating.** After a two-year lull, 16.6% of companies now meet the 4% annual reduction threshold, up from 11.0% in 2024. The median company reduced shares by 1.33% year-over-year.

4. **Earnings quality is improving.** The operating-to-GAAP gap narrowed to 20-21% in H2 2025, the tightest since 2021. Technology has the cleanest earnings at a 12.5% gap.

5. **Dividend growth has stalled.** After 12% growth in 2023, dividend payouts declined in 2024-2025 as companies favored buybacks. The index yield of 0.73% is near historical lows.

6. **Valuations are elevated but not extreme.** At 29.8x operating earnings, the market is pricing in continued growth. The 12.0% annualized 5-year return matches the long-term average, but future returns face a higher starting valuation.
